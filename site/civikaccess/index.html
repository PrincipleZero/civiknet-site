The problem

Public services were built
one program at a time.

Every program built its own forms, its own intake, its own data system. A family touching five agencies gets described five different ways. Frontline workers patch the gaps with sticky notes and personal phone calls. The work that holds the system together is invisible — and so is where it breaks.

How CivikAccess works

Build the pathway. Publish it. Update it from the field.

Three things CivikAccess does that the field has been missing.

01

Build pathway coordination

Design the operating model with your network. Define who owns each step. Set what closes a handoff. Map how a family moves from one agency to the next.

02

Publish the live pathway

The working pathway is visible to everyone in the network. See where families are stuck. See where the system is reliable — and where it isn't.

03

Update from the field

Case managers propose pathway changes when reality shifts. Recurring updates get elevated into the standard. The workers who proposed them get credit.

Privacy by architecture

Coordination without identification

A family can be supported through an entire pathway — across multiple agencies — without their personal identifying information ever entering CivikAccess in a form that can be surfaced, queried, or extracted. Personal data stays inside the agency that already holds it; the platform routes the work, not the person. Read the full privacy architecture.
